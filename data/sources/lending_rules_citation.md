# lending_rules.json — Source Citations

## Data Sources
- FCA Mortgage Market Review (MMR) — PS12/16 (2012): mandatory stress testing + affordability
- Bank of England Financial Policy Committee LTV limits guidance
- Individual lender published product criteria (verified against lender websites, Q1 2026)
- UK Finance Mortgage Lending Standards
- FCA Consumer Credit Data (mortgage lending statistics)

## Methodology
- Multipliers sourced from lender published criteria pages and broker-facing criteria guides
- Stress rate: FCA MMR requires higher of (contract rate + 1%) or notional rate (min 6.5%)
- Self-employed: SA302 + 2 years accounts is the standard across all major lenders
- Metro Bank: 3-year accounts required (stricter than standard)
- Specialist lenders (Kensington, Precise): 1-year accounts accepted for established applicants
- MT Finance: Bridging lender — not income-multiple assessed

## Coverage
10 lenders covered: Barclays, HSBC, Lloyds (incl. Halifax), NatWest (incl. RBS), Santander, Metro Bank, Kensington, Precise, MT Finance.

## Tier Definitions (Income Brackets)
| Tier | Annual Income | Description |
|------|--------------|-------------|
| Basic | £0–25K | Minimum / near-minimum wage |
| Standard | £25–75K | Typical employed single income |
| Mid | £50–75K | Established professional |
| Upper-Mid | £75–100K | Senior professional / dual income |
| High | £100K+ | High earner / complex income |

## Stress Rate Ranges
- Standard lenders: 6.5% (per FCA notional floor)
- HSBC / Metro Bank: ~7.0% (contract rate + higher buffer)
- Specialist lenders (Kensington, Precise): 7.0%+

## Known Limitations
- Multipliers are published caps; actual approved multiples depend on full affordability assessment
- High-earner multipliers (max tier) require underwriter review and clean credit profile
- Bonus/OT treatment varies by lender and is subject to employment tenure requirements
- BTL rental coverage: 125% minimum is standard; Metro Bank uses 130%
